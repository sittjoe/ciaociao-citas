# Ciao Ciao Joyería — Product Context

## Product Purpose

citas.ciaociao.mx is the private appointment booking portal for Ciao Ciao Joyería's showroom. It is not a storefront — it is the first impression of an exclusive experience. Clients use it to reserve a private, one-on-one visit where they receive personal attention from the team.

The app has two surfaces:
- **Client booking flow** — the public experience a client sees from first visit to confirmation. Design IS the product here; the interface signals the quality of the jeweler before the client sets foot inside.
- **Admin panel** — the back-office tool the family business uses to review, accept, and manage appointments. Design serves efficiency here, but still reflects the brand.

## Users

**Primary (client):** Women 28–55, Mexico City, upper-middle to high income. They are gifting themselves or a loved one. They may be nervous about the exclusivity of the showroom. The interface must feel warm and confident, not cold and gatekeeping. Spanish is the primary language.

**Secondary (admin):** The Ciao Ciao family, 4 members, using the admin panel daily on a laptop. They know the system well and want speed. They trust the brand but expect a polished tool.

## Register

- **Brand** on the client-facing surface (booking flow, /invitado/[token], /reserva/[code])
- **Product** on the admin surface (/admin/**)

## Tone

- **Client:** Warm, assured, luxurious. Never corporate or clinical. Never effusive or over-exclamatory. "Tu cita está reservada." Not "¡Tu cita está lista!" Confidence without fanfare.
- **Admin:** Precise, efficient, brand-consistent. Short labels. No decorative copy.

## Anti-references

Do NOT look like:
- Tech SaaS (cream + gray, Inter everywhere, shadcn cards)
- Wedding industry (calligraphy fonts, blush + gold, confetti)
- Generic luxury e-commerce (black + gold on white, serif everything)
- Any typical appointment booking tool (square grids, clinical buttons, system colors)

## Strategic Principles

1. **Warmth before exclusivity.** The client should feel welcomed, not evaluated.
2. **Motion as signal.** Every transition communicates care and craft. Nothing pops or jumps.
3. **Type carries the luxury.** Cormorant Garamond at large scale communicates more brand than any image.
4. **No chrome, only content.** Borders and shadows recede. Typography and spacing create hierarchy.
5. **Speed for the operator.** Admin interactions should be keyboard-navigable. The family sees this panel all day.
